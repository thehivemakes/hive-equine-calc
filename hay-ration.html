How Much Hay Does a Horse Need?

The foundation of equine nutrition is forage (hay and/or pasture). A horse should consume at least 1–1.5% of its body weight in forage daily to maintain gut health and prevent ulcers and colic.

Concentrate (grain) supplements forage for working horses or those with higher energy needs. Most idle or lightly worked horses thrive on hay alone with a vitamin-mineral supplement.
