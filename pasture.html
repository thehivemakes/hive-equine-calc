How Much Pasture Per Horse?

Pasture requirements depend on climate, soil quality, rainfall, and management. In lush climates with good management, 1.5–2 acres per horse is sufficient. In dry regions, 5–10 acres may be needed.

Rotational grazing (dividing pasture into paddocks and rotating horses through them) significantly improves grass recovery and can support 25–50% more horses per acre than continuous grazing.
