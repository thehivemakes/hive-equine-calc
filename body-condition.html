How to Score Horse Body Condition

The Henneke Body Condition Scoring System rates horses from 1 (emaciated) to 9 (extremely obese) by evaluating fat deposits in six areas: neck, withers, behind the shoulder, ribs, loin, and tailhead.

A score of 5 (moderate) is ideal for most horses. The ribs should be felt with slight pressure but not visually prominent. Regular BCS assessment helps prevent obesity-related conditions like laminitis and metabolic syndrome.
